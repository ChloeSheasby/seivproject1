Tips and Tricks for Surviving CS

Computer Science is a very challenging but also very rewarding major. In order to make sure you get the most out of your time as a Computer Science major, we've compiled a list of tips that helped us and other students.

General Tips for College

Stay On Top of Homework

Seems straightforward, but it can be easy to see due dates that are far off and put off working on an assignment. The issue with this is that most classes, especially in CS, build on top of themselves. So, if you don't learn how something works, you won't be able to use that piece to build something more complicated.

Go to Class

Another tip that seems obvious, but is easy to ignore. College is a busy time with many things fighting for your attention, but don't let these pull you away from class too often. College is expensive; you want to get your moneys' worth for it.

Get Help on Homework

This is not an encouragement to copy someone else's work line-for-line. Rather, talk about what you're struggling to understand in a class. Often other students can put subjects in terms you can better understand and help you when you get stuck.

Computer Science Hacks (The Good Kind)

Your IDE is Your Best Friend

When writing code, it's important to know how to use the tools present in your Integrated Development Environment (IDE). IDEs come with many built-in features for formatting text, detecting errors, and helping with version control and debugging. Learning how to use these features will save you hours of frustration.

Get Used to Learning New Languages

Computer Science is field that is constantly evolving and diversifying. Different companies are always trying new languages to better fit their projects on top of the different languages for different development strategies. College is less about learning all the skills you need and more about teaching you how to quickly find out how to do new things.

Work on Your Own Projects

While classes will provide you with a variety of projects to work on, it is important to take the time on your own to refine your skills and try to work on problems that interest you. This will not only help you better discover what area of CS you want to work in, but it also shows potential employers that you don't just see CS as a way to make money.


Do's and Don'ts of CS

DO:

- Add comments to your code

- Take care of your laptop

- Use online resources

- Ask for help!

DON'T:

- Copy code without understanding it

- Do the bare minimum

- Get behind on work

- Be afraid to talk to other students
